<p><p>CRISPR, or Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats, is a revolutionary biotechnology tool that enables precise editing of DNA within organisms. It utilizes a guide RNA to target specific sequences in the genome, allowing for modifications that can deactivate, enhance, or alter genes. CRISPR-associated (Cas) genes, which often work in concert with CRISPR, encode proteins that facilitate the DNA editing process. This technology has transformative applications across various fields, including agriculture, therapeutics, and genetic research.</p><p>The CRISPR and CRISPR-Associated (Cas) Genes Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.1% during the forecast period, driven by increasing investments in genome editing research and rising demand for gene therapy solutions. Key trends include the development of novel CRISPR systems, advancements in delivery methods, and broader applications in personalized medicine. The market is also witnessing collaborations between academic institutions and biotechnology companies, fostering innovation. Moreover, the ethical debate surrounding genome editing is prompting the establishment of regulatory frameworks, which will ultimately shape market dynamics. Overall, the CRISPR market is poised for significant growth as technology matures and its applications expand.</p></p>
